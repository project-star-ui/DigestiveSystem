The Mouth: Where It All Begins!

When you take a bite of food, your mouth goes to work right away!

  • Teeth: Chop and grind food into little pieces
  • Tongue: Moves food around and tastes it
  • Saliva: Special spit that makes food slippery

Fun Fact!

Your mouth makes about 1 to 2 liters of saliva every day - that's a whole bottle of soda!

The Esophagus: Food's Waterslide!

After you swallow, food takes a quick ride down the esophagus!

  • 10 inches long - about as long as your school ruler
  • Muscles push food down in waves (like squeezing toothpaste)
  • Takes just 2-3 seconds to reach your stomach

Did You Know?

You can swallow upside down because the esophagus muscles work against gravity!

The Stomach: The Food Mixer!

Your stomach is like a stretchy food bag with superpowers!

  • Stretchy muscles squeeze and mix food
  • Special juices break food into smaller pieces
  • Works for 2-4 hours on each meal
  • Has a protective lining so it doesn't digest itself!

Small Intestine: Nutrient Highway!

This is where all the good stuff from your food gets absorbed!

  • Super long tube (22 feet!) curled up in your belly
  • Tiny villi fingers grab all the vitamins and nutrients
  • It takes 3-6 hours for food to travel through
  • Most digestion happens here

Wow!

If you spread out all the villi in your small intestine, they would cover a tennis court!

Large Intestine: The Water Saver!

This is the last stop on our food journey!

  • Removes water from leftover food
  • Home to good bacteria that help keep you healthy
  • The last 5 feet of the digestive system
  • Takes 12-24 hours to do its job

Fun Fact!

Your gut has about 100 trillion bacteria - that's 10 times more than all the stars in our galaxy!
